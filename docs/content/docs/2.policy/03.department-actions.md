## Inter-Departmental Coordination

Dark Sky Conservation success depends on systematic coordination across departments. Three mechanisms ensure effective collaboration:

### 1. Steering Committee

**Chair**: Secretary, Environment & Climate Change Department
**Members**: Secretaries of Tourism, Forest, Energy, Urban Development, Public Works, Public Health, Agriculture, Law, Tribal Development, Finance, Education
**Member-Secretary**: AstronEra (Technical Secretariat)

**Functions**:
- Quarterly progress monitoring
- Budget allocation decisions
- Policy refinement based on pilot findings
- Inter-departmental dispute resolution
- National and international liaison

**Meeting Frequency**: Quarterly (minimum), with ad-hoc meetings as needed

### 2. Technical Working Groups

Three specialized working groups handle cross-departmental technical issues:

**Tourism & Economy Working Group**:
- Members: Tourism, Finance, Tribal Development, Education
- Focus: ROI analysis, marketing strategy, community benefits, training programs
- Output: Astrotourism development plans

**Ecology & Health Working Group**:
- Members: Forest, Environment, Public Health, Agriculture, Fisheries
- Focus: Monitoring protocols, impact assessment, research coordination
- Output: Scientific evidence base for policy refinement

**Energy & Infrastructure Working Group**:
- Members: Energy, Urban Development, Public Works
- Focus: Retrofit specifications, procurement standards, grid integration
- Output: Technical standards and implementation guidelines

### 3. Technical Secretariat: AstronEra

**Role**: Provide secretariat services, technical guidance, DSI liaison, training coordination, data management, policy translation

**Key Functions**:
- Secretariat services to Steering Committee and Working Groups
- Technical liaison with DarkSky International
- Dark Sky Place certification coordination
- AstroGuide training program management
- Night-sky quality monitoring and data management
- Policy brief preparation for departments
- International best practice research
- Stakeholder engagement facilitation

## Implementation Timeline

### Phase I: Pilot (2026-2029)

**Year 1 (2026)**:
- Steering Committee formation (Q1)
- 11 pilot sites selected (Q2)
- Baseline data collection begins (Q3)
- AstroGuide training launches (Q4)

**Year 2 (2027)**:
- Draft ALAN notification released for consultation (Q1)
- First pilot site lighting retrofits (Q2-Q4)
- First DSI certification applications submitted (Q3)

**Year 3 (2028-2029)**:
- Data analysis and policy refinement (Q1-Q2)
- Stakeholder consultations on notification (Q3)
- ALAN notification finalization (Q4)
- 3-year pilot monitoring report (Q4)

**Deliverables**: 11 operational pilot sites, draft ALAN notification, trained department staff, baseline data, ROI validation

### Phase II: Scaling (2029-2035)

**Years 4-5 (2029-2030)**:
- ALAN notification issued (2029 Q1)
- Statewide rollout begins by zone type (Q2)
- EIA integration (Q3)
- Municipal lighting retrofits accelerate (Q4)

**Years 6-8 (2031-2033)**:
- All Municipal Corporations adopt Model Lighting Ordinance
- Protected Area Management Plans updated
- Enforcement systems fully operational
- 50+ dark sky sites certified

**Years 9-10 (2034-2035)**:
- Mid-phase evaluation
- Policy amendments based on scaling experience
- Regional collaboration with neighboring states
- National policy input

**Deliverables**: Statewide ALAN regulations, 50+ certified sites, 30% energy reduction, measurable biodiversity recovery

### Phase III: Consolidation (2035-2047)

**2035-2040**:
- National policy leadership
- Neighboring state replication support
- Academic research centers established
- International recognition

**2040-2047**:
- Long-term ecological monitoring
- Climate impact documentation
- Maharashtra as global dark sky exemplar
- 100+ certified sites operational

**Deliverables**: National Dark Sky Conservation Act (Maharashtra model), 100+ certified sites, documented health and climate benefits
